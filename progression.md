in the after action screen the player gets their rewards and can spend it on upgrades.

First there is a round where each container which was recovered but not beaconed is shown in an animation where the player needs to hold spacebar, the container juggles dramatically and then breaks open, revealing what was inside. Then the contents of these boxes are added to the identified and recovered ones and the resources fly from the list to the players resource counter in the top right of the screen.

The resource counter has a number and an icon for each resource showing what they have stored thus far.

after all the resources have flown up to the counter, the list is cleared and three cards are shown. They slide in from the bottom, and then one by one they flip over to reveal an upgrade.

The possible upgrades are declared in the config file. Each upgrade has a name text, description text, effected turret or car stat, and an amount or modifier added. Each upgrade also has a cost in resources. An upgrade can cost one or more of one or more types of resource. The three upgrades are random, but only pick from upgrades that are possible for the player to purchase.

The player can click an upgrade to buy it. It might be possible for the player to buy more than one. 

The player can then play a new train round by clicking a bottom centered button that says "Next Raid".

For now the players progress is not saved when they quit.
